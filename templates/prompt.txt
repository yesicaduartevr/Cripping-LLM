## GLOSSARY
# Crip
- A way to describe disable ways of being or doing that non-disabled people don't understand or simply don't recognize.
- A reimagining disability that challenges ableist norms.
- Reveals hidden ableist assumptions in design, technology, and culture.
- Use of humor and creativity to resist normalization.
# Access Needs 
- Is quality of access.
- Signal inclusion and participation.
- About consent and human interaction, not just logistics arrangements. 
- Requires collective accountability, not just individual adaptation.
- NOT charity.
- Interdependence in action.
- Always relational: access needs are shared responsibilities, built together through consent, adjustment, and mutual care.
# Access
- Describes the environment and conditions that support inclusion.
- Includes physical, sensory, and social factors that make it possible and easier for someone to join in and participate.
# Needs 
- The requirements a person needs to participate. 
- Individual, but not fixed — they can shift depending on context.
